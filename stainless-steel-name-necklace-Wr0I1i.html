Understanding Your Prescription Details


Your prescription contains vital information that online retailers use to craft your lenses. Key terms you'll encounter include:


    
    
  • SPH (Sphere): Indicates the lens power for farsightedness (+) or nearsightedness (-).
    • 
    
  • CYL (Cylinder): Measures the lens power needed for astigmatism.
    • 
    
  • AXIS: Defines the orientation of the astigmatism correction on a 1 to 180-degree scale.
    • 
    
  • ADD (Addition): For multifocal or progressive lenses, indicating the added magnifying power for reading.
    • 
    
  • PRISM: Less common, corrects eye alignment issues.
    • 
    
  • PD (Pupillary Distance): The distance between the centers of your pupils.
    • 



Ensuring you accurately input these details is paramount for the optical performance of your new online eyeglasses. While some platforms may offer preliminary vision screenings or guidance on can you get an eye exam online for glasses?, a comprehensive eye exam from a licensed optometrist is always recommended to ensure your prescription is current and all eye health concerns are addressed. How to measure PD for online glasses?


Your Pupillary Distance (PD) is arguably one of the most critical measurements when you buy prescription glasses online, as it ensures the optical center of your lenses aligns precisely with the center of your pupils. A correct PD prevents eye strain and ensures clear vision.


Here are simple ways to measure your PD at home:


    
    
  1. With a Ruler: Stand about 8 inches from a mirror. Place a ruler horizontally above your eyes, aligning the 0 mm mark with the center of one pupil. Read the measurement at the center of the other pupil. Repeat a few times for accuracy.
    2. 
    
  2. With a Friend: Have a friend stand about an arm's length away. Ask them to place a ruler across your forehead, just above your eyes. Have them measure the distance from the center of one pupil to the center of the other.
    3. 
    
  3. Using an App: Several smartphone apps are designed to measure PD using your phone's camera. While convenient, always double-check these measurements against a manual method if possible.
    4. 



Most online retailers allow you to input a single PD number (binocular PD) or two numbers (monocular PD for each eye). Knowing how to measure PD for online glasses? empowers you to confidently complete your order for perfectly aligned online prescription glasses.
