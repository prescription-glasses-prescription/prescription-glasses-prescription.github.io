The Critical **Measure pupillary distance for glasses online**

Beyond your optical prescription, the pupillary distance (PD) is an equally critical measurement that online retailers need to correctly manufacture your `online prescription eyewear`.

Why PD is Indispensable and Often Missing

The pupillary distance (PD) is the measurement, in millimeters, between the centers of your pupils. This seemingly small detail is "needed to ensure that you're looking through the optimum part of the lens" for accurate vision correction. If your PD is incorrect, the optical center of your lenses won't align with your pupils, leading to blurry vision, eye strain, headaches, and a generally uncomfortable experience with your **prescription glasses on line**.

Annoyingly, "opticians have no obligation to measure your pupillary distance" during a routine eye exam, nor are they required to include it on your written prescription. This oversight often means that customers purchasing `internet-purchased spectacles` online will need to perform self-measurement, which adds an extra step to the process but is vital for a correct fit.

Practical Methods for Self-Measuring PD

Fortunately, measuring your PD yourself for your **prescription glasses on line** is straightforward. Alternatively, you can measure your PD yourself "using a ruler" with assistance from a friend or family member. Stand about 8 inches away from a mirror or directly in front of your helper.
